Who

I was born in Helsinki, Finland in 1994. I'm an observer personality. I learn by looking, touching and doing. I love to build stuff with my own hands. I gravitate towards good and honest intentions. I wish to join communities that are like families where everyone feels they can be themselves.

Workhistory

When I was 20 I decided to teach myself how to code. I started with ASP NET, Ruby on Rails, React and Nodejs. After one and a half years of living and breathing code, I had 2 real world projects under my belt. My first project was a reservation web app for a local yoga studio. The second one was a sauna reservation web app for my parents' housing company. That one is still alive and in use every week.

That is when I got introduced to Slush. I was the tech lead / CTO of Slush for three years. I learned most of my real technical skills at Slush.

Skills

General

At Slush I led at most a seven headed team. We created several frontend and backend apps. We talked with customers and learned how to get feedback. As Slush is held once a year, we deployed our tools to other conferences like HEL Tech and Stockholm Tech Fest to shorten the feedback loop. We also utilized some tools like Hotjar and UsabilityHub to gain feedback and test our hypothesis.

I learned how to take care of my team and communicate with other teams. I learned a lot of respect and a lot from life.

I learned to design complex systems with a team. I learned a great deal about how people tend to use web tools.

In production

At Slush we utilized the Google Cloud Platform to most of our deployments. We hosted a GraphQL API Gateway on App engine. We hosted some Dockerized microservices on Kubernetes. We deployed them with Circle CI. We used Google Cloud Pub/Sub for asynchronous service communication. We hosted general functionality, triggers and CRON jobs on Cloud Functions. We used Natural Language API to extract keywords of the companies attending Slush, and indexed them for excellent searchability through Algolia in our matchmaking tool. For data analysis we used Metabase with a Postgres database. Through the data at Slush I have also learned skills in Postgres and SQL in general.

All of our apps were written with Nodejs. All frontends with Angular. Backends with Express and Nestjs.

We used Mongodb, Postgres, Firestore, Firebase realtime database, Algolia, Contentful and Elasticsearch as our production databases.
